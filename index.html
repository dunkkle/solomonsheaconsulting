FAQ

Common questions

Can't we just use ChatGPT?

Sure, and for plenty of things you should. ChatGPT is great at answering questions, but it can't move data between your CRM and your accounting system at 2am, and it won't sit with your team until they trust a new process. That's the part you're paying for.

What happens to our data?

Your data stays in your own systems. When a workflow does call an AI model, it goes through business-tier APIs that don't retain anything and don't train on your data. If a process is sensitive enough that you don't want it touching the cloud at all, I can set up an open-source model that runs entirely on your own hardware.

Who owns what you build?

You do. Everything gets deployed in your accounts, so if we ever part ways it all keeps running and it's all yours.

Is this about replacing my staff?

No. The point is to let the team you already have handle the workload of a bigger one. If the plan is to automate your way into layoffs, we're probably not a fit.

What does it cost?

Less than the hire you've been putting off. A loaded admin salary in Orange County runs $65k to $90k a year, and most of my clients spend a fraction of that to get the same hours back.

Bring me your messiest process.

The first call is thirty minutes and free. Tell me about the process everyone in your office hates, and I'll tell you whether it can be automated and roughly what that would be worth. If the honest answer is that it isn't worth automating, you'll hear that too.
