FAQ

Common questions

Do we need to know what we want automated?

No. Most clients know where the frustration is, not what the fix should be. Finding the best starting point is part of the job; it's what the free consult is for.

Can't we just use ChatGPT?

Sure, and for plenty of individual tasks you should. A business workflow is different: it has to connect to your systems, follow your rules, handle the exceptions, and run when nobody's sitting there prompting it. Making that dependable, and getting your team to trust it, is what you're paying for.

What happens to our data?

Your data stays in systems and accounts you control. When a workflow uses an AI model, I use business-grade services configured so your data isn't used for training, with retention kept to the minimum the provider allows. And if something is sensitive enough that you don't want it touching the cloud at all, I can set up an open-source model that runs on your own hardware.

Who owns what you build?

You do. Everything gets deployed in your accounts, with the documentation and access to run it. If we ever part ways, it all keeps working and it's all yours.

Is this about replacing my staff?

The goal is to help the team you already have handle more, make fewer errors, and spend less time on repetitive admin. Automation can also help you put off the next administrative hire. What I don't do is design projects around layoffs.

What don't you do?

Websites, marketing, IT support, custom apps for resale, and AI strategy decks with nothing built. If what you need is one of those, I'll point you to someone good.

What does it cost?

Fixed-price projects start at $7,500; ongoing partnerships at $3,000 a month. The right comparison is the hire you're putting off: a loaded admin salary in Orange County runs $65k to $90k a year, and most clients spend a fraction of that to get the hours back.

Bring me your messiest process.

The first call is thirty minutes and free. Tell me what your team keeps doing by hand, and I'll tell you whether it can be automated and roughly what that would be worth. If the honest answer is that it isn't worth automating, you'll hear that too.
